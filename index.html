Get to know your jeans size — Kickoff

Shopping for jeans in the store when you are not sure about your size can be time-consuming and frustrating. It’s hard to compare different styles, and embarrassing/slow have to ask for a measure type or for some staff to help you with the size when you are not sure. The case study proposal is designing a solution to help people immediately find the right jean size in a store, without them having to use a measuring tape.

Personas — Emphatise phase

1

GOALS Use the least time to select the right jeans and purchase. Find jeans that fit and are comfortable Help me to remember or know my jeans size. Help me to filter casuals and work ones.

2

BEHAVIOURS Like to go to the store, she has one close to her office and used to go in her lunch break. Always forget her jeans size. Like to see how jeans would look like on her but she is lazy to try. Like to share with friends.

3

NEEDS An easy way to know her size and remember it. Personalised way of seeing jeans styles. Feel confident before buying Easy share with friends.

Challenges— Discovery phase

1

Personal fit (size) Different sizes for women, men and children Size systems: US International size (from XS to XXL), UK sizes (from 6 to 24 ) Europe sizes (from 32 to 40 women, and until 50 men), Measurements types ( cm/inches) Jeans types: variants depend on fit and rise. Jogging, skinny, Boyfriend, regular, boot cut, loose fit, high-wasted, original rise, regular rise, medium rise, Low Rise, Ultra low.

2

Determining the jeans size Measurements needed: Waistband and leg or step length. Possible to use the height, weight, belly shape and age of a person. Is there any system to help? Is the system capable of error-proof the image captured? Can the system reduce user-friction? Does the system inform the user in all the needed actions?

3

After having the jeans size Different styles fits. Any system that helps in the process?

Brainstorming — Define phase

1

3D body scanning and augmented reality System used in fitness and sport, aesthetics, medical weight loss and also made to measure. Analysed hundred of body measurements, extract measurements like your waist, hip, thigh, and more with less than 1% error.  Chart your key measurements over time. Set preferences, and manage your account from anywhere. Together with augmented reality to help tried different fits (skinny, loose etc..)

2

Personalised interactive journey(example Asos system https://www.fitanalytics.com/) Asking the customer for height, weight, then for belly size etc… Could be made as an stand alone, question answer, interactive

3

OCR technology Enable to scan any document where you have your measurements and bring you a suggestion of possible size.

Prioritise, choose idea — Define phase

1

3D body scanning and augmented reality The preferred option to a holistic experience. The 3D body scanning is easy to use and bring quick (aprox. 35 s.) accurate measurements. Together with the augmented reality would be possible for the user to see herself with some of the trousers. The main disadvantage is the cost of the System

2

Fit- analytics system Not as accurate and fast as the 3D scanning but easy to implement and cheaper

3

OCR technology Relies too much in the user having certain Documents in hand, not a suitable solution for this case. It would not work for everyone.

Methodology

An initial research for curtains and blinds configurator was conducted with the aim of making an informed decision about which of the 3 previously mentioned options was the best to move forward with.

12 moderated UT (user testing)sessions were conducted. 6 for desktop, 6 for mobile devices

We spent 60 minutes with customers:

-Opening interview to discuss triggers for getting curtains and/or blinds, needs, key decisions and expectations for instore experience versus online one(20 minutes)

-Observing and testing user behaviour whilst exploring and interacting with the functionality proposed in the prototypes as well as 247blinds.co.uk and johnlewis.com for choosing and selecting curtains and blinds. The aim of also testing with the user 2 competitor sites was in the case of John Lewis to discover what was the utility of the visualiser from a customer point of view (30 minutes)

-Closing interview to rationalise between what users said and what they did(10 minutes)

Emoti-C-on — Kickoff

1

The National Autistic Society estimates that there are around 700,000 people on the autistic spectrum in UK more than 1 in 100 people. Families included, autism is a part of daily life for 2.8 million people. Autistic children tend to have communication problems and challenges in expressing their emotions. Teachers in various autistic schools admit finding it challenging to get and maintain the attention of their students. These teachers also have limited teaching tools (e.g. PECS and TEACCH) most of which are paper-based. There is, therefore, a gap in the market for technology-enhanced teaching tools like the Emoti-C-on application.

Emoti-C-on Overview

2

The project would be launched as an e-learning app/ platform for teachers' use to enhance social skills and empathy in autistic children. Focused mainly on emotions, it is a highly customizable application which provides a personalized learning plan, empowering children to take an active part in their own learning. It involves pairing illustrated emotions using a facial expression recognition system with the appropriate scenarios. The app also enhances positive social reinforcement, as it provides some gaming experience in which the children have to coordinate and communicate their actions with a partner.

Research activity

1

The application is targeted at teachers of autistic pre-school children - given the importance of early childhood development (emotionally and otherwise). In future the application could be extended to cater for later development years.

2

Our market research revealed that, although there is a considerable number of applications targeted at autistic children, one is yet to find any application focused on helping teachers / autistic children achieve their teaching/learning objectives.

3

The infographic on the University of Edinburgh site (http://www.dart.ed.ac.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/app-wheel-April15.pdf) gives an overview of popular apps for autistic users and shows highlights the lack of tools for teachers of autistic children.

KU Extra — Overview

1

In the context of the CfGE project, students are the main users.

2

The aim is to create a platform/ service they connect with and will enjoy using.

3

The purpose of the focus group sessions was to understand students:

  1. Motivations for HE study (at KU)
  2. Current awareness of and engagement (or lack thereof) with existing co-curricular/ extracurricular programmes.
  3. use of technology.

KU Extra — Focus groups discussion: Aspirations and motivations

When asked about what they want to get out of their studies and the career opportunities they believe their course offered:

Most(over 90%) of the participants said they want good employment on graduation.

Some students (mainly L4) were unsure of their career prospects and wanted more guidance (from their faculties) on this.

Awareness and Engagement

  1. 50% of the participants were aware of at least one KU co-curricular initiative listed on the slide they were shown.
  2. On average, students indicated a patchy engagement with KU co-curricular initiatives.

Redesign — insurance App

Work carried out while working at Halo Insurance (the company no longer exists) which merged with Covermore. The aim was to move from an obsolete, difficult-to-use insurance app to a more modern, user-friendly one.
Simple imaginary emphasises the call to action. The process of getting a policy quote follows a conversational experience wherein the user proceeds through a question-answer standalone process, thereby enhancing the experience's humaneness. Dynamic content where the user can see greyed out the next question but doesn't become active until fill the first. The process of uncluttering the screens allows the user to concentrate on the task at hand while also enhancing their awareness of the process. The user can see complete information about each choice and even change it by scrolling up and down.
