Flyer

For this project, I worked mainly in line with the existing brand. Throughout the process, I have always been looking at their website in order to create a flyer according to the design on their web, a modern, clean and adaptive design easily portable to different formats.

I have created several options, and I would like to show you 3 of them. However, if I had to choose I would pick the first one. I think it is more modern than the rest and all the information is set on one page engaging the viewer in a unique and easy way. I have used the brand's colours to further ingrain their message, considering colour as a means to expose the message I am promoting.

I have added icons to convey the message and make the text more entertaining, and I have also tried to follow the principle of `less is moreÂ´ so as to inspire readers with just about enough information to get them to want know more about the company. I have finally used a call to action, encouraging the audience to discover the website and get them involved more closely in the process.

The difference between the first and last options is that, while both convey a more modern design than the second one, the last one has a back and front image, making it more expensive to print.

On the other hand, taking into account the printing process, I have produced them to be printed on 150 gr matte coated paper. The method used could be both digital or offset, depending on the number of flyers.
